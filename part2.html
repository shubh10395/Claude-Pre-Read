The anatomy of a great prompt

A prompt is more than a question. The best prompts give Claude four things: a role to inhabit, context about the situation, a clear task, and a format for the response. You don't always need all four ‚Äî but knowing about them makes you a better prompter.

Vague vs. specific ‚Äî the biggest lever

Specificity is the single highest-leverage change you can make to a prompt. A vague prompt forces Claude to guess your intent. A specific prompt tells it exactly who the audience is, what success looks like, and what constraints matter.

Common mistake: People write short prompts because they feel like they're being demanding by writing more. The opposite is true ‚Äî more context = less guesswork = better output. Claude doesn't charge by the word.

Control the format of responses

Claude will match your format if you tell it what you want. Need bullet points? Ask. Want a numbered list? Say so. Prefer a short paragraph over a long essay? Specify. You're in control ‚Äî you just have to ask explicitly.
