Claude is not a search engine

The most common mistake people make with Claude is treating it like Google. They type short, keyword-style queries and wonder why the output feels shallow. Claude isn't retrieving information ‚Äî it's generating a response based on what you give it.

This distinction matters more than it sounds. A search engine finds existing pages. Claude constructs a new response, word by word, based on your entire message ‚Äî its training, and everything in your conversation so far.

Claude has a memory limit

Every conversation with Claude has a context window ‚Äî the maximum amount of text it can "see" at once. Think of it like a whiteboard: Claude can only work with what's written on it. Old messages can fall off the edge as conversations get long.

The context window includes everything: your messages, Claude's responses, any files or documents you share, and your system instructions. Understanding this helps you plan longer work sessions.
